# Week 2 Narrative Brief

Week 2 is not only a plotting week. It is a five-figure narrative week.

The deliverable is:

- five figures
- one clear question per figure
- six short paragraphs of prose that tie the figures into one story

## Core Rule

The figures are the deliverable.

Your code, CSVs, and intermediate tables support the argument, but the reader
sees the figures and the prose around them.

## Paragraph Structure

Use this six-paragraph structure for the U.S. practice narrative:

1. opening paragraph: one sentence per figure, naming the question each one answers
2. monetary-policy and Treasury-rates paragraph
3. equity-returns and wealth-growth paragraph
4. policy-rate versus labour-market paragraph
5. equity-versus-volatility paragraph
6. closing paragraph: what does the economy look like at the end of the sample?

The Australia lecture pack is the model. The U.S. pack is the student practice.

## Figure Sequence Rule

Each figure should answer one sub-question of the larger story.

Example:

- Figure 1: what did rates do?
- Figure 2: where was the inversion episode?
- Figure 3: what happened to wealth growth?
- Figure 4: what relationship shows up between policy and labour?
- Figure 5: what relationship shows up between returns and volatility?

## Week 2 Writing Rules

- active voice
- sentence titles and sentence captions
- no modelling claims unless the task explicitly moves beyond Week 2
- no forecasts in the Week 2 narrative pack
- name the source and sample window in the figure or caption, not only in prose

## Australia Lecture Narrative

The Australia pack uses this sequence:

1. the cash-rate cycle
2. the inflation regime
3. the labour market
4. the external sector
5. the endpoint comparison

The closing claim should read like a macro case summary, not a model result.

## U.S. Practice Reminder

For the chapter-aligned U.S. practice, the intended frozen teaching window is
`2015-01-01` through `2025-12-31`.

The Week 2 FRED scripts now default to that frozen lecture window. If you
switch to `--rolling-window` for fresher data, make sure your prose and figure
captions match the updated sample before you submit or present.
